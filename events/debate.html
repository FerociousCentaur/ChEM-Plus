ABOUT:

Real engineering problems are rarely black and white. It is particularly true when problems are placed on the canvas of societal, economic, ethical, environmental, and political considerations.
There are, however, few (if any) places in the standard undergraduate curriculum to discuss and debate complex interconnected issues, exploring the pros and cons of various positions. With that in mind, we at Chem+ are willing to conduct a Chemical Engineering Debate Competition, in which we will be having a heated discussion among the brightest minds, on different Chemical Engineering problems and the ways to curb them.

EVENT DETAILS:

The contest will be held in two rounds.

ROUND 1 :(depends on the no. of registrations):
Students will be given a topic to write an essay on in round 1. Selected students will be shortlisted for the second round to be held virtually.

ROUND 2 :
The second round will be a proper debating contest. Each team will be having 2-3 members depending on the no. of registrations.
Maximum Number of Team Members: 3 and Minimum Number of Team Members: 2

In the end, the judge will analyze the arguments presented and, based on that, will declare the winners. Note that we may have two speakers per team, which will depend on the no. of registrations.

There will be two teams:

● Affirmative Team:
■ Speaker1 (Time limit:3 min): Will define keywords, introduce the team’s argument and begin the debate.
■ Speaker2 (Time limit:2min): Will continue the argument and rebut the other team’s argument.
■ Speaker3: (Time limit:2min): Will further continue and rebut other team’s arguments and summarize their discussion.

● Negative Team:
■ Speaker 1 (Time limit:3 min): Will define keywords, introduce the team’s argument and can rebut the Affirmative team’s speaker1’s arguments.
■ Speaker 2 (Time limit:2min): Will continue the argument and rebut the other team’s argument.
■ Speaker 3: (Time limit:2min): Will further continue and rebut other team’s arguments and summarize their discussion as well as the debate.
